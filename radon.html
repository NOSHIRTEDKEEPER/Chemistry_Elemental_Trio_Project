Radon (Rn)

Group 18 · Period 6 · p‑block · noble gas · radioactive

Discovery & basic data

History (RSC): Discovered in 1900 by Friedrich Ernst Dorn (Germany) while studying radium decay; called it “radium emanation”. Independently isolated by Ramsay and Soddy (1903).
Statistics: Atomic number 86 · Mass of most stable isotope 222Rn 222.0176 u · No stable isotopes; 222Rn (half‑life 3.823 d) is the longest‑lived natural isotope.

Position & origin

Location: Group 18 (noble gases), period 6, p‑block.
Origin: Continuously produced by radioactive decay of radium (uranium/thorium series). Found in trace amounts in soil, groundwater, and air.

Physical properties

  • State at 20 °C: colourless, odourless gas (glows yellow/red when cooled).
  • Density: 9.73 g/L at STP – heaviest known gas.
  • Melting point: −71 °C.
  • Boiling point: −61.7 °C.
  • Radioactivity: alpha emitter (hazardous).

Chemical properties

  • Reactivity: Noble gas – extremely inert under normal conditions. Forms no stable compounds at RT; under extreme conditions (e.g. fluorine at high T) may form RnF₂ (predicted).
  • Configuration: [Xe] 4f¹⁴ 5d¹⁰ 6s² 6p⁶ (closed shell). Full configuration: 1s² 2s² 2p⁶ 3s² 3p⁶ 3d¹⁰ 4s² 4p⁶ 4d¹⁰ 4f¹⁴ 5s² 5p⁶ 5d¹⁰ 6s² 6p⁶
  • Electronegativity: not applicable (no stable compounds).
  • Ionisation energy: 1037 kJ/mol (first).

Bonding & ions

Bonding: Monatomic; only van der Waals forces between atoms.
Ions: No stable ions in solution. Rn⁺ and Rn²⁺ have been detected in gas phase mass spectrometry.

Applications & hazard

  • Radiotherapy (historical): Used in sealed seeds for cancer treatment (now largely replaced).
  • Earthquake prediction: Monitoring radon in groundwater may signal seismic activity.
  • Environmental hazard: Second leading cause of lung cancer (after smoking) – accumulates in poorly ventilated buildings from soil.
  • Scientific research: Tracer for atmospheric studies.

Name

From radium because it is produced by radium decay. Originally called “nitron” (Ramsay), but “radon” became IUPAC standard in 1923.
