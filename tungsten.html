Tungsten (W)

Group 6 · Period 6 · d‑block · transition metal

Discovery & basic data

History (RSC): 1781 – Scheele identified an oxide from scheelite. 1783 – Elhuyar brothers reduced WO₃ with carbon to isolate the metal. Symbol W from wolframite.
Statistics: Atomic number 74 · Mass 183.84 u · Stable isotopes: ¹⁸²W, ¹⁸⁴W, ¹⁸⁶W (¹⁸⁴W most abundant). CAS 7440-33-7.

Position & origin

Location: Group 6, period 6, d‑block.
Principal ores: scheelite (CaWO₄) and wolframite [(Fe,Mn)WO₄]. Major producers: China, Russia, Vietnam.

Physical properties

  • State at 20 °C: solid, silver.
  • Density: 19.3 g·cm⁻³.
  • Melting point: 3414 °C (highest of all metals).
  • Boiling point: 5555 °C.
  • Hardness: Mohs ~7.5 (pure metal).

Chemical properties

  • Reactivity: Inert at RT; resists O₂, H₂O, most acids. Soluble in HF/HNO₃.
  • Configuration: [Xe] 4f¹⁴ 5d⁴ 6s². Full configuration: 1s² 2s² 2p⁶ 3s² 3p⁶ 4s² 3d¹⁰ 4p⁶ 5s² 4d¹⁰ 5p⁶ 6s² 4f¹⁴ 5d⁴
  • Electronegativity: 2.36 (Pauling).
  • Ionisation energies (kJ/mol): 770, 1700, 2300.

Bonding & ions

Bonding: Metallic (strong, high m.p.).
Oxidation states: −2 to +6; most stable +6 (WO₃, [WO₄]²⁻).
Biological role (RSC): Heaviest element used by life – in bacterial enzymes for aldehyde synthesis.

Applications

  • Alloys: High‑speed steels, superalloys, furnace parts.
  • Tungsten carbide (WC): Cutting/drilling tools, mining.
  • Lighting: Historically filaments; Ca/Mg tungstates as phosphors.
  • High‑density uses: Ballast, aircraft, armour penetrators.
  • Catalysis: WO₃, sulfides in petrochemical and environmental processes.

Name & symbol

W from wolfram (older name); tungsten (Swedish “heavy stone”) is now IUPAC official. Symbol W remains historical.
